Looking at the graphs, there seems to be a link between the number of fires and the temperature. Higher average temps correlate with more fires. Lower average temperatures and a large amount of damage done by the fires line up surprisingly closely.

At first I thought that might be because the large fires meant that the staff taking the data would have to leave, causing a void in data during the hotter summer months. However, looking at the null data, that is not the case. My parents suggested that it might be that the smoke and ash from the fires led to lower temperatures. I don't have the smoke and ash data to figure out if that's the case.
