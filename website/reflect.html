I feel that gathering and calculating the data went very well. I later found I could have gotten older data but 1970 seems a good cutoff point. I also think my averaging was accurate.

One thing I wish could have been done is a map of each individual province. However, that would have been time consuming and I decided to spend my time elsewhere.

I think this definitely connects with how simplifing data can make it hard to see what is really going on, shown in class in Seeing Like a State by James Scott. This is best shown with the drop in temperature around the time there are large and damaging fires. The only thing my data could show was that that result is not connected to data being missing. My best guess is what my parents suggested, that smoke and ash from the fires led to a reduction in temperatures. However, because I focused on just the temperature and rainfall data, I can't support or deny that. I think I would need data on ash and smoke from the fires and whether those would affect temperature to find out.
