I feel that gather and calculating the data went very well. I later found I could have gone with more but 1970 seems a good cutoff point. I also think my averaging was accurate.

One thing I wish could have been done is a map and each individual province. I didn't do this because google sheets was going to be a pain in the neck to make 4 graphs for each province/territory and I decided to spend my time elsewhere.

I think this definetly connects with how simplifing data can make it hard to see what is really going on, shown in class in Seeing Like a State by James Scott. This is best shown with the drop in temperature around the time there is a lot of damage from fires. The only thing my data could show was that its not connected to data being missing. My best guess is what my parents suggested, that smoke and ash from the fires led to a reduction in temperatures. However because I focused on just the temperature and rainfall as far as weather goes I can't support or deny that. I think I would need data on ash and smoke from the fires and whether those would affect temperature to find out.
